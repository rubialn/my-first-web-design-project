Overview

What is SDG 4?

Goal 4 aims to provide accessible and high-quality education for everyone at every stage of life.

To reduce inequities and disparities in education, SDG • 4 recognises that education is crucial for enabling upward socioeconomic mobility. It can open doors for:

  • Access: reducing gaps for children in poverty, rural areas, conflict zones and migrant communities.
  • Quality: improving teaching, school infrastructure and learning materials.
  • Lifelong learning: supporting skills for work, active citizenship and digital participation.

For SDG • 4 to be achieved globally, government and partners need to prioritise education funding, support teacher training and expand safe, inclusive learning spaces.

Targets

Target 4.1

Free, equitable and quality primary and secondary education

Target 4.1 is about making sure every child can finish free, safe and high-quality primary and secondary school, and acctually learn to read, write and use maths with confidence.

  • Strengthen basic schooling: invest in public schools, teachers and learning materials so that classrooms are not overcrowded.
  • Focus on real learning: track whether students are gaining core skills, not just moving from grade to grade.
  • Close the gap: support learners in rural areas, conflic zones and low-income communities so they are not left behind by 2030.

Target 4.2

Early childhood development and pre-primary education

Target 4.2 recognises that the earliest years shape a child's brain, hearlth and confidence. Quality early childhood care and pre-primary education give children a strong start before they ever walk into primary school.

  • Early year matter: play-based learning, stories and games help children build language, curiosity and social skills.
  • Support for families: parents and caregivers need access to affordable childcare, nutrition and health services.
  • Ready for school: when children attend pre-primary, they are more likely to stay in school and succeed later on.

Target 4.3

Equal access to affordable technical, vocational and higher education

Target 4.3 focuses on giving young people and adults fair access to techincal training, vocational courses and higher education, without cost becoming an impossible barrier.

  • Open door to TVET and college: expand places in technical institutes, apprenticeships and universities for learners from every background.
  • Make study affordable: use scholarships, grants and flexible fees so students are not forced to drop out because of money.
  • Support lifelong skills: create pathwats for adults to reskill and upskill as technology and labour markets change.
